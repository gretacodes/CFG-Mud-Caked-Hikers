LONDON'S BEST WALKING ROUTES

Jubilee Greenway

The Jubilee Greenway was completed in 2012 to mark the Queen's Diamond Jubilee and the London 2012 Games.This is a fairly recent addition to London’s walking routes. It is 60km long - one km for each year of the Queen's reign. It was created to link together all the major Games sites. This route allows you to see so many of London’s main attractions including Buckingham Palace, the Oympic Park, Regents park and Camden market.

The line sculpture trail

London’s first official and permanent public art walk was launched: The Line, a trail of sculptures running along three miles of waterways. It’s a trail that never gets old as the artwork is changed frequently. This walk explores not only vast amount of art but also allows you to discover London’s docklands.

Hampstead Heath Circular Walk

If what you are in the mood for is to step out of the hustle and bustle of London and be surrounded by nature then this is the walk for you. The Heath has over 30 ponds, huge bright rhododendron bushes bursting with colour, and a vast variety of wildlife.
